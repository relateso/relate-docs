## Overview

Relate supports a full, **bi-directional email sync** between Relate and your email service (Google Mail, Outlook, etc.). This means you can send and receive emails directly from Relate, and all emails sent and received from your email service are synced to Relate.

## Choosing a connection method

All email connections start from the [Integrations](https://admin.relate.so/services) page in the Relate Admin. Depending on your email service, connect using one of the three methods below.

![Integrations page](/images/setup/integrations-services.png)

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Gmail" icon="google" href="#connect-gmail">
    Connect a Google / Google Workspace account via OAuth.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Outlook" icon="microsoft" href="#connect-outlook">
    Connect an Outlook / Office365 account. (Admin approval may be required.)
  </Card>
  <Card title="IMAP / SMTP" icon="envelope" href="#connect-via-imap">
    Connect any other email service (Yahoo, self-hosted, etc.) via IMAP/SMTP.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Info>
  Regardless of the method, once the account is connected you must complete the final **[Creating connections](#creating-connections)** step so the email actually syncs in your Workspace.
</Info>

<Tip>
  If Two-factor Authentication (2FA/MFA) is set on your email account, temporarily disable it or use an App Password before connecting. See the [FAQ](#frequently-asked-questions) for details.
</Tip>

## Connect Gmail

On the [Integrations](https://admin.relate.so/services) page, select the **Google** card.

Follow the prompts to sign in with your Google account and grant permissions to complete the connection.

![Google auth](/images/setup/google-auth.png)

Once connected, the account appears under **Connected accounts**, where you can toggle **Emails · Contacts · Calendars** individually.

![Connected Google account](/images/setup/email-connected.png)

Now proceed to [Creating connections](#creating-connections).

## Connect Outlook

On the [Integrations](https://admin.relate.so/services) page, select the **MS (Outlook)** card.

Follow the prompts to sign in with your Microsoft (Office365 / Outlook) account and grant permissions to complete the connection.

Once connected, proceed to [Creating connections](#creating-connections).

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="If Office365 requires admin approval" icon="user-shield">
    When connecting an Office365 account, your organization's admin may need to approve the app. Follow the steps below.

    1. Sign in to [Office365](http://www.microsoft.com/) with an admin account.
    2. Go to **More > Admin > Show all > Settings > Org settings**.
    3. Select **User consent to apps** and check whether it is enabled. If disabled, enable it and save.
    4. Retry the Office365 connection. When the **Permissions requested** screen appears, select `Accept` to finish.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Connect via IMAP

Email services other than Gmail and Outlook (Yahoo, self-hosted, etc.) connect directly via IMAP/SMTP.

<Warning>
  If you have already connected through Google or Outlook, skip this step and go to [Creating connections](#creating-connections).
</Warning>

### 1. Start the IMAP connection

On the [Integrations](https://admin.relate.so/services) page, select the **IMAP** card, then click **`Connect`**.

![Start the IMAP connection](/images/setup/imap-connect.png)

### 2. Select the provider

When the login screen appears, click **`View all providers`** at the bottom and choose **IMAP** from the provider list.

![Provider select](/images/setup/email-provider-select.png)

![Choose IMAP from the provider list](/images/setup/email-provider-list.png)

### 3. Enter your credentials and IMAP/SMTP server info

On the IMAP login screen, enter your **email address and password**. Then expand **`Server Information`** and enter the **IMAP Host / SMTP Host** and their **Port** (defaults: IMAP `993`, SMTP `587`), and click **`Next`**.

![Enter IMAP/SMTP information](/images/setup/imap-smtp-form.png)

<Tip>
  After entering your email address, click **`Autodetect Settings`** to auto-fill the server info when possible. If auto-detection fails or the values are wrong, enter the server values manually. Check **`Different SMTP credentials`** if your outgoing server uses separate login details.
</Tip>

IMAP/SMTP host addresses and Port/SSL values differ per email service. This information is usually found on your email service's **admin page or customer support articles**.

Once the status changes to **Connected** on the Relate page, the account is connected. Now proceed to [Creating connections](#creating-connections).

<Tip>
  If Two-factor Authentication (2FA/MFA) is set on your email account, temporarily disable it before completing the procedure.
</Tip>

## Creating connections

After connecting your email account, connect it to a Workspace so the sync actually begins.

![Creating connections](/images/setup/creating-connections-workspaces.png)

Go to the [Admin](https://admin.relate.so/) page of the Workspace you want to connect, navigate to [Integrations](https://admin.relate.so/workspaces/integrations) > [Email](https://admin.relate.so/workspaces/integrations/email), and click the `Connect to workspace` button.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I've connected my email but I don't see any emails in the app.">
    Try refreshing the app first with Cmd+Shift+R (Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows). Depending on the amount of email being synced at once, the process can take anywhere from 5 minutes to up to 8 hours.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Changing my email password disconnected the integration.">
    Changing your email account password disconnects the email connection. In that case, reconnect your email by following the connection steps above.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I use Two-factor Authentication (2FA/MFA) — how do I connect?">
    When 2FA/MFA is set, temporarily disable it before connecting with Relate, then connect again.

    If your email service (Outlook, O365, iCloud, Yahoo!, etc.) supports App Passwords, you can use an App Password instead of your regular password.

    Links to set up App Passwords for each email service:

    - [Microsoft](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/account-billing/using-app-passwords-with-apps-that-don-t-support-two-step-verification-5896ed9b-4263-e681-128a-a6f2979a7944)
    - [Yahoo!](https://help.yahoo.com/kb/learn-generate-password-sln15241.html)
    - [iCloud](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204397)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
